# Role Alignment Guide
## Support Engineering Manager vs. Customer Support Tier 2 vs. PS Engineering Manager

This guide helps interviewers understand the differences between these three roles and diagnose which is the best fit for Douglas (or any candidate).

---

## Three Paths at Istari

### Path 1: Support Engineering Manager
**Direct reports**: 2 TSEs  
**Customer interaction**: Strategic + escalation  
**Technical depth needed**: Broad (troubleshoot across all systems, but not deepest)  
**Success metrics**: Team satisfaction, SLA compliance, CSAT, issue recurrence rate, team retention

**Best for**: People who love building systems, scaling teams, creating processes that last

**Day-to-day**:
- 1-on-1s with TSEs
- Mentoring on tough issues
- SLA/process tuning
- Customer escalations (high-stakes only)
- Quarterly retrospectives and process improvements

**Energized by**: Team growth, system design, seeing TSEs level up

**Drained by**: Not being hands-on with customers, repetitive operational tasks

**Ideal balance**: 70% strategy/management, 30% hands-on troubleshooting

---

### Path 2: Customer Support Tier 2
**Direct reports**: None  
**Customer interaction**: Direct, deep, ongoing  
**Technical depth needed**: Very deep (be the expert customers rely on)  
**Success metrics**: First-contact resolution, customer satisfaction, reputation for expertise

**Best for**: Deep problem-solvers who want technical mastery and direct customer impact

**Day-to-day**:
- Investigating complex customer issues
- Writing detailed troubleshooting guides
- Becoming expert in specific domains (K8s, AWS, observability, etc.)
- Teaching other TSEs through runbooks and deep dives
- Building relationships with power-user customers

**Energized by**: "Aha!" moments solving hard problems, customer gratitude, technical depth

**Drained by**: Meetings, process work, people management, context-switching

**Ideal balance**: 85% hands-on troubleshooting, 15% documentation/knowledge sharing

---

### Path 3: Professional Services Engineering Manager
**Direct reports**: 1–3 PS engineers (or IC to start)  
**Customer interaction**: Very high, ongoing partnerships  
**Technical depth needed**: Deep (architect customer solutions, hands-on implementation)  
**Success metrics**: Project delivery on-time, customer success, revenue/renewal rate, reference-ability

**Best for**: Technical leaders who want customer partnerships, architecture, and delivery responsibility

**Day-to-day**:
- Scoping customer implementations (pre-sales support)
- Designing custom solutions
- Hands-on implementation with customers
- Managing project timelines and budgets
- Building deep customer relationships
- Travel to customer sites (regular)

**Energized by**: Customer partnership, delivery milestones hit, seeing customers succeed with their solution, revenue impact

**Drained by**: Purely internal work, hands-off from customers, not being technical

**Ideal balance**: 50% hands-on engineering, 50% project/people leadership

## Key Diagnostic Questions

### Q1: "What energizes you most at work?"

| Answer | Suggests |
|--------|----------|
| "Seeing my team level up" / "Building processes" / "Scaling the support function" | **Manager** |
| "Solving gnarly technical problems" / "Being the go-to expert" / "Customer gratitude" | **Tier 2 Support** |
| "Customer success" / "Delivering a complex project" / "Architect and implement solutions" | **PS Engineering** |

---

### Q2: "What's your ideal hands-on vs. strategic work split?"

| Answer | Suggests |
|--------|----------|
| "70% strategic, 30% hands-on" | **Manager** |
| "90% hands-on, 10% strategic" | **Tier 2 Support** |
| "50-50" | **PS Engineering** |

---

### Q3: "Tell me about a project you loved. What made it satisfying?"

| Answer | Suggests |
|--------|----------|
| "Founded the Support function" / "Scaled team" / "Built SLA process" | **Manager** |
| "Took a 1,000-bug backlog and resolved 90%" / "Diagnosed a production outage no one else could" | **Tier 2 Support** |
| "Delivered a complex customer implementation" / "Architected a custom integration" | **PS Engineering** |

---

### Q4: "How do you feel about customer relationships?"

| Answer | Suggests |
|--------|----------|
| "I like them, but I prefer managing through my team" | **Manager** |
| "I love deep relationships with power users but prefer async (email/tickets)" | **Tier 2 Support** |
| "I love customer relationships, especially in-person. The relationship IS the job." | **PS Engineering** |

---

### Q5: "What does 'success' look like to you?"

| Answer | Suggests |
|--------|----------|
| "My team is happy, retained, and growing" / "SLAs are met, recurring issues drop 50%" | **Manager** |
| "Complex problems I've solved" / "Reputation as the expert" / "Customers trust me" | **Tier 2 Support** |
| "Successful customer implementations" / "Repeat business, customers become references" / "Revenue impact" | **PS Engineering** |

## Red Flags by Path

### Manager Red Flags
- "I don't want to manage people" → Wrong path
- "I want to be hands-on all the time" → Frustrated in this role
- "Customer service is boring" → This role touches customers strategically
- "I don't care about team development" → Core of the job

### Tier 2 Support Red Flags
- "I want to move into management eventually" → Set expectations: IC track is equally valued
- "I'm tired of technical work" → Might be burnt out, not a good fit
- "I want to travel and meet customers" → Maybe PS Engineering?
- "I don't enjoy deep technical work" → Not the right path

### PS Engineering Manager Red Flags
- "I don't want to travel" → Role requires customer site visits
- "I prefer not to deal with customers" → Core of the job
- "I want pure hands-on coding with no project management" → IC Engineer, not PS Manager
- "I'm not interested in revenue or business outcomes" → Role has $ accountability

## Interviewer Tips

### If you're hearing Manager signals:
- Dig deeper: "What did you love about founding the function vs. what was hardest?"
- Probe team development: "Tell me about someone you developed who exceeded expectations"
- Understand strategic thinking: "How did you know when to hire vs. when to automate?"

### If you're hearing Tier 2 Support signals:
- Dig deeper: "You mentioned resolving 90% of the bug backlog. What was the most complex issue?"
- Test technical depth: "How would you approach a tricky database performance issue?"
- Understand motivation: "Would you want to manage people, or stay hands-on?"

### If you're hearing PS Engineering signals:
- Dig deeper: "What's your favorite customer project you've delivered?"
- Test architecture skills: "Design a solution for this customer scenario..."
- Understand customer love: "Do you prefer email support or in-person customer visits?"

## Decision Framework

After the interview, ask yourself:

**Is this person primarily energized by**:
- **Building systems & developing people?** → **Manager**
- **Technical mastery & solving hard problems?** → **Tier 2 Support**
- **Customer partnership & project delivery?** → **PS Engineering**

If the answer is mixed (e.g., "I'd be happy in any role"), probe further:
- "If you had to choose just one, which appeals to you most?"
- "Which would frustrate you most: no hands-on technical work? No customer relationships? No team to develop?"

## Role Transitions (Future)

This isn't a one-way street. Typical career paths:

```
Tier 2 Support → Manager (+ team oversight)
Manager → Director (larger team) or VP (strategic)
Tier 2 Support → PS Engineering (add customer touch)
PS Engineering Manager → VP Professional Services (strategy + revenue)
```

Douglas's path at Harvest was:
- IC Engineer → Support Engineer (founded function) → Manager (scaled team) → VP (100+ org)

**His natural trajectory**: Manager or Director-track.
