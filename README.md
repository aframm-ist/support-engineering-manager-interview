## Role Overview

**Title**: Support Engineering Manager (Software Product)

**Reports To**: VP Engineering

**Team**: 2 Technical Support Engineers (initially)

**Key Responsibilities**:
- Lead and develop 2 TSEs
- Own support tooling (Pylon configuration, SLA design, routing)
- Drive metrics and reporting (SLAs, CSAT, recurring issues)
- Create and maintain runbooks and knowledge base
- Product feedback loop (quarterly digests, release testing, customer insights)
- Maintain quality gate between customers and engineering

**Required Experience**:
- 7+ years in technical support or solutions engineering
- Prior team management experience
- Deep familiarity with: Kubernetes, Docker, PostgreSQL, observability (Prometheus/Grafana), REST/GraphQL APIs, Linux, networking/TLS

**Nice-to-haves**:
- Mechanical engineering or simulation domain knowledge
- CAD, FEA, CFD, PLM/PDM experience
- Defense or government contracting background
- Kubernetes certifications (CKA, CKS)

## Evaluation Criteria

### Four Dimensions (1–5 scale each)

| Dimension | Target | What It Measures |
|-----------|--------|------------------|
| **Technical** | ≥3 | Hands-on K8s, observability, troubleshooting depth |
| **Leadership** | ≥4 | Hiring, developing, accountability, difficult conversations |
| **Product Thinking** | ≥3 | Feedback collection, customer advocacy, product sense |
| **Istari Fit** | ≥4 | Alignment with mission, excitement, realistic expectations |

**Recommendation Framework**:
- All four ≥ threshold → **Strong Yes**
- 3 of 4 ≥ threshold → **Yes** (if the gap is addressable)
- 2 or fewer ≥ threshold → **No** or **Maybe**

## Tips for Success

### Before You Interview

1. Read the resume carefully. Understand their background.
2. Identify 2–3 questions you're particularly interested in based on their experience.
3. Prepare your own story about Istari and why you work here.
4. Have a real customer example ready (if relevant to the question).
5. Set phone to Do Not Disturb.

### During the Interview

1. **Listen more than you talk.** They should speak ~70% of the time.
2. **Take notes** with direct quotes; it helps calibration later.
3. **Ask follow-ups.** "Tell me more about that" is completely fair.
4. **Don't sell the role.** You're evaluating first.
5. **Stay neutral.** Don't challenge or argue with their answers.

### After the Interview

1. Complete `CANDIDATE_EVAL_TEMPLATE.md` within 30 minutes while it's fresh.
2. Sync with other interviewers if multiple people interviewed.
3. Discuss red flags and calibrate on the recommendation.
4. Identify what you'd ask in a follow-up, if needed.

---

## Red Flags

- **Weak communication**: Can't clearly articulate their thinking or experience
- **Shallow technical depth**: No hands-on Kubernetes, troubleshooting, or observability experience
- **Limited leadership track record**: No clear examples of hiring, developing, or managing people
- **Transactional view of support**: "Fix the ticket and move on" vs. "How do we prevent this?"
- **Doesn't ask about Istari**: No curiosity about the product, customers, or team
- **Avoids accountability**: Blames others, doesn't own decisions
- **Unwilling to learn**: Says "I don't know" and stops, instead of "Here's how I'd approach it"

## How to Use These Materials

### Scenario 1: First interview with this role
1. Read `QUICK_REFERENCE.md` (5 min)
2. Skim `INTERVIEW_GUIDE.md` (15 min)
3. Conduct interview (2.5 hours)
4. Complete `CANDIDATE_EVAL_TEMPLATE.md` (15 min)
5. Sync with hiring manager or other interviewers

### Scenario 2: Preparing a panel of interviewers
1. Everyone reads `QUICK_REFERENCE.md` and `INTERVIEWER_CALIBRATION.md` (20 min)
2. Hold a 15-minute calibration call to align on "what's strong?"
3. Each interviewer focuses on 1–2 sections of `INTERVIEW_GUIDE.md` (to avoid repeating questions)
4. Conduct interviews separately
5. Sync for debrief and recommendation

### Scenario 3: You're the hiring manager, calibrating assessments from multiple interviewers
1. Read all completed `CANDIDATE_EVAL_TEMPLATE.md` forms
2. Check for consistency: Did all interviewers rate Technical similarly?
3. If ratings diverge, discuss why (different expectations? Red flag they missed?)
4. Use `INTERVIEWER_CALIBRATION.md` to align on "what strong looks like"
